Getting Started & Resources

Welcome to Imagine's Coding Villa! You've come to the right place for development; whether you're new to programming with zero experience, or a skilled developer who wants to help others. This guide will give you everything you need to know in order to succeed while using our help channels.

Below are a few websites and servers that will help you with your development journey:

  • Discord.js beginners' guide
  • Discord.js documentation
  • MDN JavaScript guide
  • Discord.js official server

Guidelines

  • Whether you're a helper or someone receiving help, server rules apply at all times (this should be obvious)
  • Never share your bot's token. If someone gets access to it, they are capable of destroying your bot and server. If it does get shared, head to the Discord Developer Portal and regenerate it.
  • Do not help people in direct messages (instead use threads), this way moderators can verify if a thank is legit.
  • Don't waste time saying "Hey can I get help" and waiting for a response, instead describe what you need help with and provide screenshots (if necessary).
    • Here's a perfect example of what not to do when you need help:

      In this example, I did show a screenshot, however it was a completely useless image of an "error" that people often show. The actual error for you to show will be above these useless logs, and that is what you should send in the channel.

  • Don't ping people for help just because their roles are hoisted, be patient and wait for help like everyone else.
  • Use code blocks when sending bits of code, by using backticks (```js <code>```). This makes codes more readable and keeps the channel clean.
